# OctoAcme Project Management Processes

OctoAcme employs a structured, iterative approach to project management, emphasizing customer value, clear roles, and data-driven decision making across all product initiatives. The lifecycle starts with project initiation, where business needs are validated, stakeholders are identified, and project objectives are articulated through a lightweight one-pager. Once approved, projects undergo detailed planning with prioritized backlogs, estimation, and risk identification, culminating in a release and delivery roadmap. The execution phase utilizes a project board with defined workflow stages ("Backlog," "Ready," "In Progress," etc.), encouraging small pull requests, automated CI/CD, and regular demos or reviews at the end of each sprint or milestone.

Core roles within OctoAcme's process include Project Managers (PMs) who coordinate delivery, manage risks, and facilitate documentation and communication; Product Managers (PdMs) who define problems, prioritize roadmaps, and validate solutions; and Developers who design, implement, and test features aligned with acceptance criteria. QA/Testers are integral, ensuring that quality benchmarks are met before release. Clear ownership is established for every project, and role responsibilities extend to risk identification, planning, and continuous improvement.

Communication is intentional and frequent, featuring daily standups focused on progress and blockers, weekly delivery syncs for updates and risk review, and monthly updates for stakeholders. Incident or escalation communication follows defined paths, with templates ensuring clarity during risks or outages. Key artifacts—such as project charters, backlogs, retrospectives, and risk registers—are maintained as living documents within the project repository.

Quality assurance is embedded throughout the lifecycle. All new logic requires unit tests, with integration and end-to-end smoke testing for critical flows. Automated CI enforces linting, testing, and security scanning prior to code review. Manual QA is performed as needed before releases, and deployment is guided by thorough pre-release and rollback checklists. Continuous improvement is fostered through regular retrospectives, with actionable insights tracked and reviewed for lasting process refinement.
